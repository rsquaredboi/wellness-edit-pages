I know now that every single one of those thoughts was wrong. But at the time, they felt like walls.

What Actually Happened (Day by Day)

Day 1: The Unboxing
It arrived in a completely plain brown box. No branding, no product names. I could have received a phone charger. The relief was immediate. Inside, the actual packaging was premium — magnetic lid, little sachets of cleaning solution and lubricant. It came fully charged. The device itself was smaller than I expected, fit in my palm, and looked more like a skincare tool than anything else.
Day 2: The First Try
Let's be honest — I locked the bathroom door, ran the shower for noise cover (unnecessary, it turned out — this thing is genuinely silent), and started at level 1. The sensation was nothing like I expected. No buzzing, no vibrating. Instead: gentle, rhythmic pulses of air. By level 3, I understood what 16,000 women had been trying to describe. It took less than four minutes.
Day 5: The Text to My Friend
"WHY DIDN'T YOU TELL ME SOONER." All caps. Three exclamation marks. She sent back a laughing emoji and "told you so." This was the moment I went from skeptic to evangelist.
Week 3: The Conversation with My Husband
I was terrified to bring it up. I thought he'd feel inadequate or weird about it. Instead, he was curious. We tried it together, and — I'm going to spare the details — but let's just say he now asks me to grab it. It enhanced what was already good and made what was occasionally routine genuinely exciting again.
The part nobody warns you about: you start recommending it to your friends.

By the end of the first month, I had texted the product link to three different friends. Two of them bought it. One texted me back at midnight: "I hate you for not telling me sooner." The cycle continues.
